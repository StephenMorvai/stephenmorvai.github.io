Matching Experiment on Kongregate

A tile matching where you get to decide how many different different tiles there are, how big the playing area is, and how many tiles need to match. My eventual goal with this game is to implement statistics gathering to see which combinations provide the optimal difficulty.


Quantum Leap on Kongregate

Quantum Leap is a game where the player controls an electron on a hydrogen atom and gets hit by photons which change the energy level of the electron. The player must try to avoid getting knocked off the electron entirely.


Gravity Simulator on Khan Academy

An interactive simulator which allows students on Khan Academy to learn about gravity and orbits while flying a space ship in an earth moon system.


BlackBerry Playbook Educational App

A BlackBerry Playbook App developed with a partner based on criteria given by elementary school students. The students could not decide on a single game so they gave us three mini games to develop. In the first game the player helps a penguin jump to across the water by answering basic math problems. In the second game the player guides an eagle through the air trying to avoid trees and other obstacles while chasing mice and fish. In the last game the player helps a cat drop apples on a mouse and gets more apples by answering math questions.
